How to Use the
Marketing Asset Builder
Build App Store graphics, social media posts, email banners, and web headers for any Tapcart merchant — in minutes, not hours. This guide walks you through every feature from loading an app to exporting your final assets.
🗺️
Overview

The Marketing Asset Builder is a browser-based design tool that pulls live screenshots from any Tapcart merchant app and lets you assemble them into polished marketing graphics across five asset types. Everything runs in the browser — no installs, no Figma, no Canva.

📸
Live Screenshot Capture
Enter any Tapcart App ID to automatically capture real screens from the merchant's live app — home, PDP, PLP, cart, and more.
🎨
8 Layout Templates
Choose from pre-built layout templates per asset type. Each template positions the phone, text, and background differently for instant variety.
✏️
Full Customization
Edit headline copy, colors, fonts, overlays, phone scale and position. Drag text blocks and phone mockups anywhere on the canvas.
💾
Save & Resume
Export your full project as a .mab.json file and reload it later. Saves all screenshots, settings, and positions.
🌍
Multi-Language
Switch the UI language from the flag dropdown in the top bar — useful when building assets for international merchants.
↩️
Undo / Redo
Full undo/redo history for all design changes. Use the buttons in the top bar or ⌘Z / ⌘⇧Z.
1
Load an App

Start by entering the merchant's Tapcart App ID in the top of the left panel. This connects the tool to the merchant's app so you can capture real screenshots.

1
Find the App ID
The App ID is found in the Tapcart dashboard under App Settings. It looks like a 10-character alphanumeric string, e.g. mLMFK4fI6j.
2
Enter the ID and optionally a name
Paste the App ID into the first field in Zone 1 of the left panel. Type an app/brand name in the second field — this will be used to pre-fill the Export filename.
3
Click Load (or press Enter)
The tool will connect to the screenshot service and begin capturing screens. Zone 1 switches to "loaded" state showing the app identity.
💡
Shopify Integration
If the merchant has Shopify credentials configured, a Collection and Product selector will appear in Zone 1, letting you capture PLP and PDP screens for a specific collection or product.
2
Capture Screenshots

Once an app is loaded, the screenshot library in Zone 2 populates automatically. You can also upload your own screenshots or capture additional screens at any time.

1
Auto-capture on load
When you click Load, the tool automatically captures several screens from the app. They appear as thumbnails in the Screenshot Library (Zone 2) as they come in — no need to wait for all of them.
2
Click a thumbnail to use it
Tap any screenshot thumbnail to set it as the active screen displayed in the phone mockup on the canvas. A yellow border highlights the selected screenshot.
3
Upload your own screenshots
Click the + Upload button next to "Screenshot Library" to add your own PNG/JPG images. These are stored in the library alongside auto-captured ones.
4
Recapture
After an app is loaded, a ↺ Recapture All button appears. Use this to refresh all screenshots if the app content has changed.
ℹ️
Delete a Screenshot
Hover over any thumbnail in the library to reveal a red ✕ button. Click it to remove that screenshot from the library.
3
Pick a Layout

Each asset type has 8 pre-built layout templates. Layouts control the overall composition: phone position, background style, text placement, and visual hierarchy.

1
Switch to the asset type you need
Use the tab group in the top bar to navigate between Email, IG Story, IG Post, Web Header, and Facebook Ad.
2
Open the Layout accordion in the left panel
In Zone 3 of the left panel, click the 🖼 Layout accordion to expand layout choices. 8 thumbnails appear showing different compositions.
3
Click a template to apply it
The canvas instantly updates to the new layout. Your existing text, colors, and screenshot selection are preserved — only the composition changes.
4
Customize Your Asset

The right panel contains all per-asset customization controls. Most changes update the canvas in real time. Many controls also support "Apply to All Screens" — look for the All toggle to apply a setting across every asset type at once.

✏️
Text & Headlines
Edit the headline and subtitle directly in the right panel. Choose font, size, weight, color, and alignment. Drag the text block on the canvas to reposition it.
🎨
Background
Choose a solid color, gradient, or upload a custom background image. Gradient controls include direction, two stop colors, and angle.
📱
Phone Mockup
Scale the phone with the size slider, adjust its position, and control shadow intensity. Drag the phone freely on the canvas. Crop sliders let you zoom in on the screenshot inside the phone.
🖼️
Frame & Overlay
Toggle the phone frame color between white, black, or a custom tint. Add a gradient or color overlay on top of the background for depth.
🏷️
App Store Badges
Toggle Apple App Store and Google Play badges on/off. Position them below the text block. Useful for any asset where you want to direct users to download the app.
🖼
Logo
Upload a merchant logo (PNG with transparency recommended). Drag it anywhere on the canvas. Control max width and height in the right panel.
💡
Apply to All Screens
Many controls in the right panel have an Apply to All option. This pushes that single setting — like background color or font — to every asset type simultaneously, keeping your set visually consistent without having to repeat changes five times.
5
Export Your Assets

When your assets look great, export them as high-quality PNGs, ready to drop into any campaign or App Store submission.

1
Export current asset
Click ⬇ Export PNG in the top bar to download the currently visible asset as a PNG file at its native resolution.
2
Export all assets at once
Click ⬇ All to download all five asset types as individual PNG files in one shot. Files are named by asset type and app name.
3
Save project for later
Click 💾 Save to export a .mab.json project file. Use 📂 Load to restore it in any future session — all screenshots and settings are preserved.
📐
Asset Types & Dimensions

Switch between asset types using the tab group in the top bar. Each tab has its own canvas dimensions, layout templates, and customization options.

Email Banner
600 × 400 px

Designed for email marketing headers — wide landscape format. Great for re-engagement campaigns, launch announcements, and promotional blasts. Exports at 2× for sharp rendering in email clients on retina displays.

Instagram Story
1080 × 1920 px

Full-screen vertical format for Instagram and other Stories surfaces. Phone mockup fits naturally in the tall canvas. Best layouts lean into negative space at the top/bottom for text placement.

Instagram Post
1080 × 1080 px

Square format for Instagram Feed, Facebook, and LinkedIn posts. Balanced composition layouts work well here. Text and phone need to share the square without competing.

Web Header
1440 × 520 px

Wide-format banner for merchant or partner websites. Often used as a "Download our app" hero section. Text on left, phone floating on right is the most common and effective layout.

Facebook Ad
1200 × 628 px

Standard Facebook and Google Display Network ad dimensions. Landscape format — similar to web header but shorter. Concise headlines and clear CTAs perform best in this format.

◧
Left Panel — 4 Zones

The left panel is divided into four vertical zones. It can be toggled open/closed using the sidebar button in the top-left corner of the top bar.

1
Zone 1 — App Loader
Enter the Tapcart App ID and an optional app name. Click Load to connect and auto-capture screenshots. Once loaded, shows app identity and Recapture controls.
2
Zone 2 — Screenshot Library
Displays all captured and uploaded screenshots as a 3-column thumbnail grid. Click to select the active screen shown in the phone mockup. Hover thumbnails to reveal the delete button. Click "+ Upload" to add your own.
3
Zone 3 — Accordions
Collapsible sections for Layout templates (8 options), Brand Kit (saved brand colors), and Settings. Click any accordion header to expand/collapse it.
4
Zone 4 — Pinned Action
A pinned button at the bottom of the panel. When screenshots are loaded, this becomes a "Render All" action to apply the current screenshot to all five asset canvases at once.
◨
Right Panel — Design Controls

The right panel contains all per-asset customization controls. It's organized into collapsible groups. Changes are reflected live on the canvas as you make them.

🖊
Text Controls
Edit headline and subtitle copy. Set font family, size, weight, color, and text alignment. A drag handle on the canvas lets you freely reposition the text block.
🌈
Background Controls
Pick a solid color or enable gradient mode with two color stops and an angle. Alternatively, upload a custom background image with optional overlay on top.
📱
Phone Controls
Sliders for phone scale, horizontal offset, vertical offset, and drop shadow intensity. A crop section lets you zoom/pan within the phone screen to frame the screenshot precisely.
✨
Overlay & Frame
Add a translucent color or gradient overlay on the background layer. Toggle and tint the phone frame between white and black tones.
⚙️
Advanced Options
Expand the "Advanced" toggle at the bottom of each group to access less common controls like letter spacing, line height, shadow blur, and element-level opacity.
🔁
Apply to All
Many sliders and color pickers include an "Apply to All" option. Use this to push a change across all five asset types at once — keeping your campaign set consistent.
🎨
Brand Kit

The Brand Kit accordion in Zone 3 of the left panel stores a merchant's brand colors for quick reuse. Apply any saved color to the background, text, or overlay with a single click.

1
View saved brand colors
Expand the Brand Kit accordion. Each row shows a color swatch, its hex value, and an Apply button. The palette row at the bottom shows all colors at a glance.
2
Apply a color
Click the Apply button next to any color to set it as the current background color for the active asset. Or click the swatch to copy the hex code to your clipboard.
ℹ️
Adding Colors
Brand colors are pre-configured per deployment. If you need to add or update a merchant's brand colors, contact your team admin to update the Brand Kit configuration.
💾
Save & Load Projects

The Marketing Asset Builder has no server-side storage — all your work lives in the browser. Use the Save/Load buttons to preserve your work between sessions.

1
Save a project
Click 💾 Save in the top bar. A .mab.json file is downloaded to your computer. The filename is based on the app/merchant name and today's date.
2
Load a saved project
Click 📂 Load and select the .mab.json file. All screenshots, layout choices, and text content are fully restored. A confirmation shows how many screenshots were loaded.
⚠️
Don't Refresh Without Saving
Refreshing the page or closing the tab will erase your current session. Always click Save before closing if you want to continue later.

Tips & Tricks
💡
Build a full set in one session
Load the app once, set your design on the Email tab, then use "Apply to All" for background color, font, and overlay. Switch tabs and make minor adjustments per format. Export All when done — full campaign set in under 10 minutes.
💡
Use brand kit colors as a starting point
Open the Brand Kit accordion and apply the merchant's primary brand color as your background. This instantly creates a on-brand starting point before you've made a single manual change.
💡
Crop the screenshot for the best frame
Not every screen looks great at default zoom. Open the Phone → Crop section in the right panel and use the zoom and offset sliders to reframe the screenshot so the most important UI is centered in the phone mockup.
💡
Use the transparent logo version
Upload a PNG logo with a transparent background for the cleanest results. A white or solid-fill logo will show a rectangle against the asset background.
ℹ️
Screenshot service latency
Screenshots are captured by a cloud service in real time. Capture typically takes 5–20 seconds depending on how many screens are being loaded. Screenshots populate progressively as they complete — you don't need to wait for all of them to start designing.
